# Problem Statement & Goals

## Audience

NJIT students who want to build AI startups while completing their degree.

## Problem Statement

Entrepreneurial students are ambitious but face barriers:  

- Difficulty balancing academics with startup building.  
- Lack of structured mentorship and investor connections.  
- Overwhelmed by fragmented resources for branding, technical setup, and scaling.  
- Need clear guidance on professional assets and startup frameworks.  

**Core Problem:** Students need a structured, supportive accelerator that helps them build professional credibility, connect with mentors, and scale their startup ideas.

## Goals

- Provide tailored onboarding workflows that highlight startup-specific needs.  
- Offer guides and templates for branding, portfolio building, and investor readiness.  
- Connect students to mentors, advisors, and collaborators via Discord and CRM.  
- Ensure compliance with accessibility, SEO, and GDPR standards for professional credibility.  
- Help students graduate with portfolio-ready assets and viable startup projects.  

## Success Metrics

- % of entrepreneurial students completing onboarding and setting up LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio, and Calendly.  
- Number of startup-focused events attended (hack nights, guest speakers).  
- Mentorship connections established through CRM/Discord.  
- Portfolio sites showcasing startup projects.  
- Post-graduation surveys showing startup launches or investor interest.

# Competitor / Comparable Analysis

## 1. Y Combinator Startup School

- **Strengths:** Free resources, strong global community, investor credibility.  
- **Weaknesses:** Not tailored to NJIT students, lacks integration with academic workflows.  
- **Comparison:** Job Club offers localized, student-focused onboarding with direct community integration.

## 2. Major League Hacking (MLH) Hackathons

- **Strengths:** Hands-on project building, networking, exposure to industry mentors.  
- **Weaknesses:** Event-driven, less focus on long-term startup development.  
- **Comparison:** Job Club complements MLH by providing continuous onboarding and portfolio guidance for startups.

## 3. University Entrepreneurship Centers

- **Strengths:** Offer workshops, incubators, and networking opportunities.  
- **Weaknesses:** Generalized, not AI-specific, limited automation and portfolio integration.  
- **Comparison:** Job Club provides AI-focused, automated onboarding with startup-specific resources and community support.
