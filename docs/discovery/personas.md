# Job Club User Personas

## Target Audience

Job Club serves NJIT students interested in AI careers, ranging from beginners to advanced learners. These personas represent the primary user segments.

---

## Persona 1: Alex - The Ambitious First-Year

### Demographics
- **Age:** 19
- **Program:** Computer Science, Year 1
- **Technical Background:** Basic Python, one CS course completed
- **Goals:** Launch AI career, understand AI fundamentals, build first projects
- **Challenges:** Unclear career path, limited industry connections, imposter syndrome

### Motivations
- "I want to turn my CS degree into an AI career"
- "I need to build real experience, not just pass classes"
- "I want to learn from people already working in AI"
- "I need to stand out to recruiters"

### Pain Points
- Overwhelmed by amount of AI information online
- Not sure which AI skills are actually valuable
- No professional network
- Concerned about fitting in with more experienced students

### Behavior
- Active on Discord and messaging apps
- Learns by doing (projects over theory)
- Attends events if they fit schedule
- Needs encouragement and validation

### How Job Club Helps
- **Onboarding:** Sets clear learning path
- **Events:** Builds confidence through mentorship
- **Resources:** Provides curated learning paths
- **Portfolio:** Creates visible proof of learning

### Success Metric
- Completes onboarding
- Attends 3+ events
- Starts first AI project
- Builds LinkedIn profile

## Persona 2: Jordan - The Career Pivotter

### Demographics
- **Age:** 22
- **Program:** Information Systems, Year 3
- **Technical Background:** Intermediate - SQL, JavaScript, some Python
- **Goals:** Pivot from traditional IT to AI, advance career quickly
- **Challenges:** Limited AI experience, needs to prove capability, time constraints

### Motivations
- "AI is where the future is heading"
- "I need to develop AI skills before graduating"
- "I want to work on meaningful problems"
- "I need to be competitive in the job market"

### Pain Points
- Worried about gaps in ML knowledge
- Limited time (working part-time, coursework)
- Needs to show quick results
- Concerns about starting over in new area

### Behavior
- Driven and goal-focused
- Prefers structured learning paths
- Values mentorship and guidance
- Wants practical, job-ready skills

### How Job Club Helps
- **Onboarding:** Accelerates skill assessment and learning plan
- **Events:** Access to industry mentors for guidance
- **Resources:** Focused guides on what's actually needed for jobs
- **Portfolio:** Templates and guidance for rapid portfolio building

### Success Metric
- Completes advanced learning path
- Attends 5+ events
- Builds 2+ AI projects
- Establishes LinkedIn and GitHub presence
- Connects with mentors

## Persona 3: Sam - The Highly Motivated

### Demographics
- **Age:** 20-21
- **Program:** Computer Engineering or Math, Year 2-3
- **Technical Background:** Strong - Multi-language, algorithms, math background
- **Goals:** Become AI specialist, contribute to cutting-edge AI, research or PhD track
- **Challenges:** Need to go beyond typical curriculum, finding advanced resources

### Motivations
- "I want to truly understand how AI works at a deep level"
- "I want to contribute to AI research or work at top companies"
- "I need to build an exceptional portfolio that stands out"
- "I want to be part of the AI revolution"

### Pain Points
- Curriculum feels insufficient for their ambitions
- Hard to find advanced, current resources
- Limited access to cutting-edge projects
- Want peer group with similar ambitions

### Behavior
- Self-directed learner
- Reads papers, follows AI trends
- Contributes to open source
- Highly motivated by intellectual challenge

### How Job Club Helps
- **Resources:** Access to advanced guides and research pointers
- **Events:** Advanced workshops and mentorship with PhD-level researchers
- **Networking:** Connect with other ambitious students and professionals
- **Portfolio:** Guidance on building research-level portfolio

### Success Metric
- Completes advanced learning path within 2 weeks
- Attends advanced workshops/office hours
- Contributes to open-source AI project
- Publishes or presents research
- Connects with researchers or senior engineers

## Persona 4: Casey - The Uncertain Explorer

### Demographics
- **Age:** 19-20
- **Program:** General Computer Science, Year 1-2
- **Technical Background:** Basic - one or two CS courses
- **Goals:** Explore if AI is right for them, keep options open
- **Challenges:** Many competing interests, unclear fit, not sure about commitment

### Motivations
- "Everyone talks about AI, I should probably learn it"
- "I want to see what the job opportunities look like"
- "I'm curious but not sure if it's for me"
- "I don't want to invest too much time if it's not my thing"

### Pain Points
- Afraid of committing to something that won't stick
- Not sure if they have what it takes
- Prefer low-pressure introduction
- Many distracting opportunities

### Behavior
- Browsing and exploring
- Low commitment to single path
- Social learner (learns with peers)
- Needs encouragement and community

### How Job Club Helps
- **Onboarding:** Low-commitment initial assessment
- **Events:** Casual events and social networking
- **Resources:** Light, accessible introductory guides
- **Community:** Supportive peer group

### Success Metric
- Completes onboarding form
- Attends 2+ events
- Engages in Discord community
- Tries one beginner project

## Persona 5: Morgan - The Working Student

### Demographics
- **Age:** 22-24
- **Program:** Returning student, Evening/Online
- **Technical Background:** Variable - career changer or degree completion
- **Goals:** Complete degree while building new AI career path, balance work/school
- **Challenges:** Time constraints, juggling multiple priorities, financial pressures

### Motivations
- "I need career advancement to improve my situation"
- "AI skills will help me earn more and have better opportunities"
- "I want to plan for the next 2-3 years strategically"
- "I need to show my employer I'm serious about growth"

### Pain Points
- Very limited free time
- Can't attend events that conflict with work
- Need flexible, self-paced learning
- Budget-conscious
- Need to show quick ROI on time investment

### Behavior
- Prioritizes efficiency over depth
- Prefers recorded/on-demand content
- Weekend or evening engagement
- Transactional mindset (return on effort)

### How Job Club Helps
- **Onboarding:** Quick assessment tailored for working professionals
- **Events:** Flexible scheduling, recorded options available
- **Resources:** Efficient, on-demand learning materials
- **Portfolio:** Templates to accelerate portfolio building

### Success Metric
- Completes onboarding in under 30 minutes
- Watches 5+ resource videos
- Participates in online Discord discussions
- Builds 1-2 projects over 3-4 months
- Applies knowledge to current job

2. Attend weekly events
3. Build projects actively
4. Engage in Discord regularly
5. Iterate on portfolio monthly

### Medium Engagement Path (Jordan)
1. Complete onboarding
2. Attend biweekly events

4. Build portfolio in parallel

### Low Engagement Path (Morgan, Casey)
1. Quick onboarding assessment
2. Monthly event attendance
3. Self-paced learning
4. Quarterly progress review
5. Flexible pacing

## Design Implications

### For Onboarding
- **Offer multiple pathways:** Advanced, Standard, Explorer
- **Make it quick:** 10-15 min for quick path, 30 min for detailed
- **Ask about time availability:** Tailor recommendations

### For Events
- **Mix of formats:** In-person, hybrid, recorded
- **Various times:** Lunch sessions, evening, weekend options
- **Different levels:** Beginner workshops, advanced talks

### For Resources
- **Structured progression:** Clear learning sequences
- **Time labels:** "5-min read", "2-hour project", "advanced paper"
- **Multiple formats:** Videos, articles, templates, code examples

### For Portfolio Guidance
- **Templates provided:** LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio site templates
- **Step-by-step:** Break down into manageable pieces
- **Examples:** Show real student portfolios at different levels

### For Community
- **Safe space:** Supportive, no judgment on skill level
- **Peer groups:** Match people at similar stages
- **Mentorship:** Connect with professional mentors
- **Celebrate wins:** Share successes in Discord
