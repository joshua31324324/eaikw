# Problem Statement & Goals (AI-Curious Freshman)

## Audience

- NJIT freshmen who are curious about AI but lack professional assets and career direction.
- Students with minimal exposure to industry tools (LinkedIn, GitHub, personal portfolio, Calendly).
- Motivated learners who want structured guidance to become career-ready.

## Problem Statement

Freshman students are excited about AI careers but face several barriers:

- They don't know how to translate classroom learning into professional branding.
- They lack confidence in setting up essential career assets (LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio site).
- They feel overwhelmed by fragmented resources and need a clear, guided pipeline.
- They want community support and mentorship but donâ€™t know where to find it.

**Core Problem:**  
Freshmen need a structured, accessible, and supportive onboarding system that helps them build professional assets, connect with peers/mentors, and gain confidence in their AI career journey.

## Goals

- Provide a **guided onboarding workflow** that identifies missing assets and gives personalized checklists.
- Offer **accessible resources** (guides, templates, workshops) tailored to beginners.
- Connect students to **community channels** (Discord, events, mentoring).
- Ensure compliance with **accessibility, SEO, and GDPR standards** so the site feels professional and trustworthy.
- Help freshmen become **portfolio-ready** by the end of their first year.

## Success Metrics

- % of freshmen who complete onboarding and set up LinkedIn, GitHub, personal site, and Calendly.
- Attendance at events/workshops (tracked via analytics).
- Engagement in Discord channels (#jobclub-intros, #events, #resources).
- Improvement in Lighthouse scores (performance, accessibility, SEO).
- Student satisfaction surveys showing increased confidence in career readiness.

# Competitor / Comparable Analysis

## 1. Replit Community

- **Strengths:** Active coding community, project sharing, collaborative learning.  
- **Weaknesses:** Focused on coding practice, not professional onboarding or career assets.  
- **Comparison:** Job Club adds structured career guidance and portfolio building, which Replit lacks.

## 2. Major League Hacking (MLH)

- **Strengths:** Hackathons, workshops, strong peer community, exposure to industry mentors.  
- **Weaknesses:** Event-driven, less focus on long-term career asset development.  
- **Comparison:** Job Club complements MLH by offering continuous onboarding and professional portfolio guidance.

## 3. University Career Centers (e.g., NJIT Career Development Services)

- **Strengths:** Resume reviews, job postings, career counseling.  
- **Weaknesses:** Generalized support, not AI-specific, less emphasis on modern tools like GitHub/Calendly.  
- **Comparison:** Job Club provides AI-focused, tech-forward onboarding with automation and community integration.

---

## Summary

For the **AI-Curious Freshman**, Job Club solves the problem of fragmented resources and lack of confidence by offering:

- A **guided onboarding pipeline** with automation.
- A **community hub** integrated with Discord and events.
- **Professional asset guidance** (LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio, Calendly).
- Compliance with modern standards (SEO, accessibility, GDPR).

This makes Job Club uniquely positioned compared to Replit, MLH, and traditional career centers.
